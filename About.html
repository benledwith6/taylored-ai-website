05 About

Two founders.
One promise.

We started Tailored AI because every small-business owner we talked to was drowning in the same repetitive work — and every AI company we talked to was trying to sell them a product instead of solving the problem.

05.1 Manifesto

We don't sell software.
We replace the task.

The AI industry in 2026 is crowded with tools that promise to "empower" your team. Most don't. They sit in a tab nobody opens, generate slightly-wrong first drafts, and get billed per seat until someone remembers to cancel.

We think that model is broken for the people we care about most — the owners of small businesses who feel the payroll every two weeks.

So we built the opposite. One partner. One retainer. Custom automations built for the way your team already works, and maintained for as long as you want them running. The goal is simple and measurable: save you more money than we cost. If we don't, we unwind the retainer and give you the year back.

Read that last sentence twice. It's the only promise that matters.

05.3 How we work

Six rules we wrote down
on day one.

01

Name the job, not the magic.

"Automated billing." Not "AI-powered revenue intelligence." We call the work what it is. If we can't explain an automation in one sentence, we haven't understood the job yet.

02

Receipts over adjectives.

Hours saved, dollars saved, tasks removed. Numbers we can show you in your own books. Everything else is marketing.

03

We're on the same side of the table.

"We" and "your." Not "clients," "users," "solutions providers." A partner, not a vendor.

04

The math has to work.

If your savings haven't beaten our fee by month six, we unwind the retainer. We wrote it into every agreement before we signed our first one.

05

Stay on. Forever, if you want.

Most firms sell you a project and disappear. We stay on — one retainer, ongoing tuning, the next automation when you're ready.

06

Four clients a quarter. That's it.

We cap onboarding because attention is the product. If you're on the waitlist, you'll hear from one of us in weeks — not months.
